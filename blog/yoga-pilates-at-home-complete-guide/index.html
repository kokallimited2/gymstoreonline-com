The Complete Guide to Yoga and Pilates at Home

July 2026 · GymStore Online

Yoga and Pilates are two of the most effective low-impact exercises you can practise at home. They require minimal equipment, deliver profound benefits for flexibility, strength, posture, and mental wellbeing, and scale from absolute beginner to advanced practitioner. Whether you want to complement your gym routine or build a complete home practice, this guide covers essential equipment, product recommendations, and sample routines.

Yoga vs Pilates: Key Differences

Yoga combines physical postures, breath control, and meditation, emphasising flexibility, balance, and mind-body connection. Pilates focuses on core strength, spinal alignment, and controlled precise movements. Many people benefit from practising both — yoga for flexibility and relaxation, Pilates for core stability and posture correction.

Essential Yoga Equipment

You need very little to start yoga, but the right gear makes a real difference. A yoga mat at least 5 mm thick with a non-slip surface is your foundation. A premium extra-thick yoga mat with alignment lines is excellent for beginners. Yoga blocks help modify poses to suit your flexibility — use them to bring the floor closer in standing folds or support your hips in seated poses. A set of high-density yoga blocks costs around £10–£20. A yoga strap extends your reach in poses where your hands cannot yet meet, and a bolster is ideal for restorative yin yoga and meditation. Quality mats cost £20–£60, blocks and straps around £10–£20 each.

Essential Pilates Equipment

Mat-based Pilates needs surprisingly little gear. A Pilates mat is thicker than a yoga mat (10–15 mm) to cushion your spine during rolling movements. A thick Pilates mat with carrying straps provides the right cushioning for exercises like the Hundred and Roll-Up. Resistance bands add progressive resistance to leg and arm work — as covered in our resistance band guide, bands pair perfectly with Pilates. A set of Pilates resistance bands with multiple tensions costs £10–£20. A Pilates ring (magic circle) adds resistance between your hands, knees, or ankles for inner thighs, arms, and core work. A Pilates magic circle costs £15–£30. A foam roller is also invaluable for warm-up and recovery — a high-density foam roller costs £12–£35.

Setting Up Your Practice Space

Choose a quiet area with roughly 2 m × 2 m of floor space. Good ventilation and natural light help. A rubber gym mat underneath your practice mat protects floors and adds grip. Keep props within arm's reach so you do not break your flow.

Sample Yoga Routine (20 Minutes)

Hold each pose for 5–8 breaths. Start in Child's Pose to relax your back, then move to Cat-Cow on all fours (8–10 rounds). Flow through Downward-Facing Dog, Low Lunge on each side, Warrior II, and Triangle Pose. Finish seated with a Seated Forward Fold, then lie in Savasana for 5 minutes of rest.

Sample Pilates Routine (20 Minutes)

Focus on slow, controlled movements. Start with The Hundred (10 breath cycles / 100 pumps). Move to Roll-Up (6–8 reps), Single Leg Circles (5 each way per leg), and Rolling Like a Ball (6–8 reps). Finish with Side-Lying Leg Series (10 pulses each direction) and Shoulder Bridge (10 reps, holding each lift for 2 seconds).

Choosing Between Them

You do not have to pick one — many people practise both. If your goal is flexibility and stress reduction, start with yoga. For core strength and posture correction, begin with Pilates. A great weekly split is 2–3 yoga sessions and 2 Pilates sessions. Both complement weight training, running, and HIIT beautifully. Progression Tips

As you improve, hold yoga poses longer, explore advanced variations (Crow Pose, Headstand), and try different styles like Vinyasa or Yin. For Pilates, increase reps, add resistance bands, and try advanced exercises like the Teaser. A Pilates reformer adds even more variety — explore options on our Yoga & Pilates page.

Final Thoughts

Yoga and Pilates are among the most rewarding home practices you can adopt. A quality mat, a block or two, a strap, and a Pilates ring are all you need to begin. Start with the routines above and enjoy the journey.
